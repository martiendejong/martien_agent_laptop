## Document Types Coverage

### 1. Invoices (Facturen)
**Purpose:** Legal financial documents requiring compliance + brand excellence

**Essential Elements:**
- Invoice number (sequential, unique)
- Invoice date + due date
- Seller details (company name, address, KVK, BTW-nummer)
- Buyer details (client name, address, reference)
- Line items (description, quantity, unit price, VAT rate, total)
- Subtotal, VAT breakdown, grand total
- Payment terms and methods
- Bank details (IBAN, BIC)
- Legal footer (KVK, BTW registration)

**NL/EU Compliance:**
- BTW-nummer mandatory for EU B2B
- Reverse charge mechanism notation
- Date of supply vs invoice date
- Currency notation (EUR, € symbol placement)
- Sequential numbering system

**Design Considerations:**
- Tabular figures for amounts (monospaced numerals)
- Clear visual hierarchy (amounts stand out)
- Scannable structure (eye tracking patterns)
- Print-safe colors (readable in B&W)

### 2. Quotes (Offertes)
**Purpose:** Pre-sale documents balancing professionalism + persuasion

**Essential Elements:**
- Quote number and validity period
- Client details and project reference
- Itemized services/products
- Pricing (optional: tiered options)
- Terms and conditions summary
- Acceptance signature block
- Next steps / call to action

**Design Considerations:**
- More visual freedom than invoices
- Brand personality can shine
- Visual hierarchy guides to action
- Optional: icons, accent colors, visual separators

### 3. Proposals
**Purpose:** Persuasive documents selling vision + capability

**Structure:**
- Executive summary
- Problem statement
- Proposed solution
- Methodology/approach
- Timeline and deliverables
- Investment/pricing
- Team credentials
- Case studies/testimonials
- Appendices

**Design Considerations:**
- Narrative flow (storytelling arc)
- Visual breaks (pull quotes, callouts)
- Data visualization (charts, timelines)
- Photography or illustration (brand-aligned)
- Premium paper stock impression

### 4. Letters (Brieven)
**Purpose:** Formal correspondence maintaining brand dignity

**Types:**
- Business correspondence
- Cover letters
- Recommendation letters
- Notice letters
- Thank you letters

**Design Considerations:**
- Maximum white space
- Focus on readability
- Conservative typography
- Signature block prominence

### 5. Statements
**Purpose:** Account summaries and financial overviews

**Types:**
- Account statements
- Payment summaries
- Transaction histories

### 6. Receipts
**Purpose:** Proof of payment documents

**Design Considerations:**
- Compact format
- Clear transaction details
- Timestamp prominence

### 7. Credit Notes
**Purpose:** Invoice corrections and refunds

**Design Considerations:**
- Clear reference to original invoice
- Negative amounts clearly marked
- Reason for credit noted

## Design Systems

### System 1: Minimalist/Modern (Stripe, Linear, martiendejong.nl)

**Philosophy:** "Perfection is achieved not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away." — Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

**Characteristics:**
- Generous white space (60%+ of page)
- Sans-serif typography (Inter, Untitled Sans, Suisse Int'l)
- Monochromatic or near-monochromatic color
- Hairline rules (0.25pt - 0.5pt)
- Subtle hierarchy (size, weight, not color)
- Grid-based precision
- Restrained use of brand color (1-5% of surface area)

**Typography:**
- Single font family (3-4 weights)
- Scale: 8pt/10pt/12pt/14pt/18pt/24pt/36pt
- Line height: 1.4-1.6 for body, 1.1-1.2 for headings
- Letter spacing: -0.02em for headings, 0em for body

**Layout:**
- Asymmetric balance
- Left-aligned text (never justified)
- 8pt or 12pt grid system
- Margins: 20-25% of page width

**Color:**
- Primary: Brand color (used sparingly)
- Neutrals: 5-7 step gray scale
- Accent: Single accent for CTAs/highlights
- Background: White or off-white (#FAFAFA - #FFFFFF)

**Best For:** Tech companies, startups, modern professional services, design agencies

**Example Brands:** Stripe, Linear, Basecamp, martiendejong.nl, Vercel, Pitch

## Typography Systems

### Font Selection Criteria

**Legibility (Primary Requirement):**
- X-height > 50% of cap height
- Open apertures (a, e, c)
- Distinct characters (I, l, 1 | O, 0)
- Clear punctuation
- Readable at 9pt minimum

**Professional Tone:**
- Avoid novelty or display-only fonts for body
- Match brand personality (modern, traditional, etc.)
- Consider industry norms (law = serif, tech = sans)

**Technical Requirements:**
- Complete character set (extended Latin, numerals, symbols)
- Tabular figures (for financial documents)
- Small caps (for acronyms, emphasis)
- Multiple weights (Light, Regular, Medium, Bold minimum)
- Professional kerning and spacing

**Print vs Screen:**
- Print: Higher contrast acceptable, serifs work well
- Screen: Lower contrast safer, sans-serifs optimal
- Hybrid: Modern "reader" fonts (Charter, Mercury, FF Meta Serif)

### Top Professional Font Families

**Serif:**
1. **Garamond** (Classic, elegant, timeless) - Law, publishing
2. **Minion Pro** (Readable, versatile) - Adobe standard
3. **Mercury Text** (Modern classic) - Hoefler & Co.

6. **Baskerville** (Refined, transitional) - Professional services
7. **Charter** (Screen-optimized serif) - Hybrid documents

**Sans-Serif:**

### Typographic Hierarchy Rules

**6-Level System (Standard Documents):**

```
Level 1: Document Title
- Size: 24-36pt
- Weight: Bold or Medium
- Color: Brand color or black
- Letter spacing: -0.02em
- Line height: 1.0-1.2
- Case: Title Case or Sentence case

Level 2: Section Heading
- Size: 18-24pt
- Weight: Bold or Semibold
- Color: Black or dark gray
- Letter spacing: -0.01em
- Line height: 1.1-1.3
- Space above: 2em, below: 0.5em

Level 3: Subsection Heading
- Size: 14-18pt
- Weight: Semibold or Medium
- Color: Black
- Letter spacing: 0em
- Line height: 1.2-1.4
- Space above: 1.5em, below: 0.5em

Level 4: Body Text
- Size: 10-12pt (10pt minimum for legal)
- Weight: Regular
- Color: #1A1A1A to #333333
- Letter spacing: 0em
- Line height: 1.4-1.6
- Alignment: Left (never justified for invoices)

Level 5: Small Text (metadata, captions)
- Size: 8-10pt
- Weight: Regular or Medium
- Color: #666666 to #999999
- Letter spacing: 0em to +0.02em
- Line height: 1.3-1.5
- Case: Can be uppercase at +0.05em tracking

Level 6: Legal/Footer Text
- Size: 7-9pt
- Weight: Regular
- Color: #999999 to #CCCCCC
- Letter spacing: 0em
- Line height: 1.3-1.4
- Alignment: Centered or left
```

**Financial Document Hierarchy:**

```
Amount (Primary):
- Size: 18-24pt
- Weight: Bold
- Font: Tabular figures
- Color: Black
- Alignment: Right

Amount (Line Item):
- Size: 10-12pt
- Weight: Medium or Regular
- Font: Tabular figures
- Color: Black
- Alignment: Right

Labels:
- Size: 8-10pt
- Weight: Medium
- Color: #666666
- Case: UPPERCASE at +0.05em tracking
- Alignment: Left
```

### Line Height Systems

**Body Text:**
- Minimum: 1.4 (tight, modern)
- Standard: 1.5-1.6 (optimal readability)
- Generous: 1.7-2.0 (luxury, airy)

**Headings:**
- Large headings (>24pt): 1.0-1.2
- Medium headings (14-24pt): 1.2-1.3
- Small headings (<14pt): 1.3-1.4

**Tables/Data:**
- Compact: 1.2-1.3
- Standard: 1.4-1.5

**Calculation:**
```
Line height (pt) = Font size × Ratio
Example: 12pt × 1.5 = 18pt line height
```

### Letter Spacing Standards

**Headings:**
- Large (>36pt): -0.03em to -0.02em (tighten)
- Medium (18-36pt): -0.02em to -0.01em
- Small (<18pt): -0.01em to 0em

**Body Text:**
- Default: 0em (trust the font designer)
- Adjust only if: Tight set, large x-height, needs loosening (+0.01em max)

**Small Caps:**
- Always track out: +0.03em to +0.05em

**Uppercase:**
- Always track out: +0.05em to +0.15em (larger sizes = more tracking)

**Numbers:**
- Tabular figures: 0em (monospaced, alignment critical)
- Proportional figures: 0em (default spacing)

### Number Formatting

**Tabular vs Proportional Figures:**

```css
/* Tabular (monospaced) - for tables, invoices, financial data */
font-feature-settings: "tnum";
/* All digits same width - perfect alignment */
1,000.00
  999.99
   10.50

/* Proportional (variable width) - for running text */
font-feature-settings: "pnum";
/* Natural spacing - better for sentences */
"We sold 1,234 units in Q1 2024"
```

**When to Use:**
- **Tabular:** Invoices, tables, financial statements, data columns
- **Proportional:** Body text, narrative descriptions, headings

**Decimal Alignment:**
```
Right-align amounts with consistent decimal places:
€ 1,234.50
€   123.00
€    12.99
€     1.50
----------
€ 1,371.99
```

**Currency Symbols:**
- **Netherlands:** € 1.234,50 (space after €, point for thousands, comma for decimals)
- **US:** $1,234.50 (no space, comma for thousands, point for decimals)
- **Consistency:** Choose one style, stick to it throughout document

## Color Systems

### Brand Color Extraction

**Step 1: Identify Primary Brand Color**
- Check logo (dominant color)
- Check website header/navigation
- Check CTA buttons
- Check key visual elements

**Step 2: Identify Secondary Colors**
- Accent colors (buttons, links, highlights)
- Background colors (off-white, light gray)
- Text colors (dark gray, black)

**Step 3: Extract Exact Values**
- Use browser inspector (DevTools)
- Use color picker tools (ColorZilla, Sipapp)
- Screenshot + eyedropper in design software
- Check CSS files if accessible

**Step 4: Document Color Palette**
```
Primary:   #0052CC (Brand Blue)
Secondary: #00B8D9 (Accent Teal)
Dark:      #1A1A1A (Text)
Gray:      #666666 (Secondary text)
Light:     #F7F8F9 (Background)
White:     #FFFFFF
```

### Monochromatic Schemes

**When to Use:**
- Minimalist brands
- Professional/corporate documents
- When brand color is too bold for full document
- Invoices and financial documents

**Construction:**
```
Base color: #0052CC

Tints (add white):
10%: #1A63D6
25%: #4082E0
50%: #7FA9EA
75%: #BFD4F5

Shades (add black):
10%: #004AB8
25%: #003D99
50%: #002966
75%: #001433

Grays (desaturate):
#2A3A4A (dark)
#556677 (medium)
#889BAA (light)
#CDD6DD (very light)
```

**Application:**
- Headings: Base color or dark shade
- Body text: Dark gray (not pure black)
- Backgrounds: Tints (very light)
- Accents: Base color
- Borders: Light gray

### Accent Color Usage

**80/20/5 Rule:**
- 80% Neutral (white, off-white, light gray)
- 20% Primary brand color
- 5% Accent color

**Strategic Placement:**
- CTAs and action items
- Key amounts or totals
- Important dates
- Section dividers (sparingly)
- Highlights in tables

**Accessibility:**
- Ensure 4.5:1 contrast ratio (WCAG AA) for text
- Ensure 3:1 for large text (18pt+)
- Aim for 7:1 (WCAG AAA) for financial documents
- Never rely on color alone (use icons, labels, formatting)

### Print-Safe Colors

**CMYK Considerations:**
- Rich black: C60 M40 Y40 K100 (not K100 alone)
- Avoid RGB-only colors (neon, pure red, pure blue)
- Test conversions: RGB to CMYK can shift hues
- Stay within gamut (SWOP, Fogra39)

**Safe Color Ranges:**
- Blues: Safe (minimal shift)
- Reds: Check shift (can muddy)
- Greens: Check shift (can darken)
- Purples: Often shift significantly
- Oranges: Generally safe

**Grayscale Fallback:**
- All documents must be readable in B&W
- Test print in grayscale mode
- Use tints/shades for differentiation
- Don't rely on color for hierarchy

### Accessibility (WCAG AAA)

**Contrast Ratios:**
- Normal text (< 18pt): 7:1 minimum
- Large text (≥ 18pt or 14pt bold): 4.5:1 minimum
- UI components: 3:1 minimum

**Color Blindness:**
- Don't use red/green alone for status
- Use icons, labels, patterns
- Test with color blindness simulators
- Deuteranopia (green weakness) most common (5% males)

**Tools:**
- WebAIM Contrast Checker
- Color Oracle (simulator)
- Stark (Figma plugin)

**Safe Combinations:**
```
Excellent contrast:
- #1A1A1A on #FFFFFF (16.1:1)
- #FFFFFF on #0052CC (8.6:1)
- #333333 on #F7F8F9 (11.3:1)

Borderline (avoid for body text):
- #666666 on #FFFFFF (5.7:1) - OK for large text only
- #999999 on #FFFFFF (2.8:1) - Fails all standards
```

## Brand Analysis Methodology

### Step 1: Initial Assessment

**Gather Assets:**
1. Visit client's website
2. Take full-page screenshots (homepage, about, services)
3. Download logo files (if available)
4. Check social media profiles (consistent branding?)
5. Review existing documents (if provided)

**Quick Analysis:**
- Industry: What sector? (Tech, finance, creative, etc.)
- Tone: Professional, casual, luxury, bold?
- Target audience: B2B, B2C, enterprise, startup?
- Design era: Classic, modern, contemporary, cutting-edge?

### Step 2: Color Palette Detection

**Manual Extraction:**
```
1. Open website in browser
2. Right-click > Inspect Element
3. Find main elements:
   - Header background
   - Navigation links
   - Buttons (primary, secondary)
   - Text colors
   - Backgrounds
4. Copy hex values from CSS
5. Document in palette:
   Primary: #______
   Secondary: #______
   Dark: #______
   Light: #______
```

**Automated Tools:**
- **Coolors.co** - Generate palette from image
- **Adobe Color** - Extract from screenshot
- **ColorZilla** - Browser extension, eyedropper
- **Sipapp** - macOS color picker

**Create Palette:**
```
BRAND PALETTE: [Client Name]

Primary:     #0052CC  ■ (Logo, headers, CTAs)
Secondary:   #00B8D9  ■ (Accents, highlights)
Dark:        #1A1A1A  ■ (Headings, emphasis)
Text:        #333333  ■ (Body text)
Text Light:  #666666  ■ (Secondary text)
Border:      #E0E0E0  ■ (Dividers, rules)
Background:  #F7F8F9  ■ (Page background)
White:       #FFFFFF  ■ (Content background)

Notes:
- Primary used sparingly (5-10% of surface)
- Body text never pure black (#000000)
- Light gray background for visual interest
```

### Step 3: Typography Matching

**Identify Fonts:**
```
1. Inspect Element on headings and body text
2. Check font-family in CSS
3. Note: font-weight, font-size, line-height
4. Screenshot key typography examples
```

**Common Patterns:**
- **Single family:** Same font, multiple weights (modern, clean)
- **Serif + Sans:** Serif headings, sans body (classic, readable)
- **Sans + Sans:** Different sans families (contemporary)
- **Display + Text:** Display font for headings, text font for body

**Find Matches:**
- **Exact match:** Use same font if available
- **Similar:** Find close alternative (e.g., Helvetica → Inter)
- **System:** Use system fonts for maximum compatibility

**Examples:**
```
Website uses: SF Pro (Apple system font)
Document match: Inter, Helvetica Neue, or SF Pro if available

Website uses: Playfair Display (serif) + Open Sans
Document match: Same, or Garamond + Helvetica

Website uses: Custom brand font (e.g., Spotify Circular)
Document match: Closest alternative (e.g., Gotham, Avenir)
```

### Step 4: Design Philosophy Identification

**Minimalist Indicators:**
- Large white space (> 50% of page)
- Monochromatic or limited color
- Sans-serif typography
- Thin borders or no borders
- Simple geometric shapes
- Hidden complexity (details on hover)

**Classic Indicators:**
- Serif typography
- Symmetric layouts
- Traditional color palette (navy, burgundy, forest)
- Formal language
- Centered elements
- Decorative rules or borders

**Modern Tech Indicators:**
- Bright accent colors
- Sans-serif fonts
- Asymmetric layouts
- Gradients or geometric shapes
- Illustrations or 3D elements
- Interactive elements

**Luxury Indicators:**
- Minimal information density
- High-contrast serif or refined sans
- Monochromatic with metallic accents
- Large format
- Premium photography
- Ample negative space

### Step 5: Visual Language Translation

**Grid System:**
- Inspect website layout (12-column? 16-column? Custom?)
- Note margin sizes (narrow, medium, wide?)
- Identify padding patterns (compact, generous?)
- Extract to document: Apply similar proportions

**Spacing System:**
```
Website spacing (example):
- Sections: 80px vertical gap
- Elements: 24px vertical gap
- Components: 12px internal padding

Document translation:
- Sections: 20mm vertical space
- Elements: 6mm vertical space
- Components: 3mm internal padding

(Scale down for print, maintain proportions)
```

**Visual Elements:**
- **Icons:** Are they used? (Solid, outline, duotone?)
- **Shapes:** Geometric patterns? Organic? None?
- **Photography:** Style? (Bright, moody, B&W, lifestyle?)
- **Illustrations:** Custom? Stock? None?

**Translate to Document:**
```
Website: Bright, colorful illustrations
Document: Subtle brand-colored geometric shapes in header

Website: Photography-heavy hero sections
Document: Single brand image in header (optional, tasteful)

Website: Icon library (Feather icons, outline style)
Document: Matching icons for contact info in footer
```

### Step 6: Tone & Voice Alignment

**Website Language Analysis:**
- Formal vs casual
- Technical vs accessible
- Warm vs professional
- Concise vs descriptive

**Document Voice:**
```
Website tone: Friendly, approachable, tech-forward
Document approach:
- Use conversational headings ("Let's work together")
- Keep language clear, avoid jargon
- Add personality in small doses (e.g., payment note: "Thanks for your business!")

Website tone: Formal, authoritative, established
Document approach:
- Use traditional headings ("Payment Terms")
- Formal language throughout
- No personality, pure professionalism
```

## Quality Checklist

### Print Readiness

**Resolution:**
- [ ] All text is vector (not rasterized)
- [ ] Images minimum 300 DPI at actual size
- [ ] Logo is vector format (SVG, EPS, or AI)
- [ ] PDF exported at high quality (no compression)

**Color:**
- [ ] Colors converted to CMYK (if printing)
- [ ] Rich black used (C60 M40 Y40 K100), not K100 alone
- [ ] Spot colors defined (if using Pantone)
- [ ] Color mode consistent throughout (all CMYK or all RGB)

**Bleeds & Margins:**
- [ ] 3mm bleed on all sides (if full-bleed design)
- [ ] No critical content within 5mm of trim
- [ ] Safe margins: Minimum 15mm all sides (20mm preferred)

**Fonts:**
- [ ] All fonts embedded in PDF
- [ ] No font substitution warnings
- [ ] Consistent font rendering (not bold via stroke)

**File Export:**
- [ ] PDF/X-1a or PDF/X-4 format (for print)
- [ ] PDF/A format (for archival)
- [ ] Crops and bleed marks included (if needed)
- [ ] Flattened transparency

### PDF Compliance

**PDF/A (Archival):**
- [ ] All fonts embedded
- [ ] No encryption or password protection
- [ ] No external dependencies
- [ ] Metadata included (title, author, subject)
- [ ] Color spaces embedded

**PDF/X (Print):**
- [ ] Output intent included (e.g., Fogra39)
- [ ] All colors CMYK or spot
- [ ] Trim box and bleed box defined
- [ ] No RGB or transparency issues

**PDF/UA (Accessibility):**
- [ ] Logical reading order (tagged PDF)
- [ ] Alt text for images
- [ ] Bookmarks for navigation
- [ ] Language specified
- [ ] No security restrictions

### Accessibility

**Visual:**
- [ ] Contrast ratio 7:1 for body text (WCAG AAA)
- [ ] Contrast ratio 4.5:1 for large text
- [ ] Document readable in grayscale (print test)
- [ ] Font size minimum 9pt (10pt preferred)

**Structural:**
- [ ] Logical reading order (top to bottom, left to right)
- [ ] Headings properly hierarchical (H1, H2, H3)
- [ ] Tables have headers defined
- [ ] Lists properly formatted

**Content:**
- [ ] Color not sole means of conveying information
- [ ] Clear language (avoid jargon)
- [ ] Abbreviations spelled out on first use

### Legal Requirements (NL/EU)

**Dutch Invoice Requirements:**
- [ ] Sequential unique invoice number
- [ ] Invoice date (datum factuur)
- [ ] Date of supply/service (datum levering)
- [ ] Seller: Name, address, KVK number, BTW number
- [ ] Buyer: Name, address (BTW for B2B within EU)
- [ ] Description of goods/services
- [ ] Quantity and unit price
- [ ] VAT rate and amount per rate
- [ ] Total amount excluding VAT
- [ ] Total VAT amount
- [ ] Total amount including VAT
- [ ] Payment terms and due date
- [ ] Bank details (IBAN, BIC)

**EU B2B Invoices:**
- [ ] Buyer's VAT number (if reverse charge)
- [ ] "Reverse charge" notation (if applicable)
- [ ] EU VAT Directive compliance

**Retention Period:**
- [ ] Documents stored for 7 years minimum (NL law)
- [ ] Archival format (PDF/A recommended)

### Brand Consistency

**Visual Identity:**
- [ ] Logo used correctly (size, spacing, color)
- [ ] Brand colors match exactly (hex values confirmed)
- [ ] Typography matches brand guidelines
- [ ] Design system consistent with website

**Tone:**
- [ ] Language matches brand voice
- [ ] Formality level appropriate
- [ ] Terminology consistent with brand

**Quality:**
- [ ] No pixelated elements
- [ ] Proper alignment (everything on grid)
- [ ] Consistent spacing throughout
- [ ] No orphans or widows (typography)

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

### Over-Design Traps

**❌ Too Many Fonts:**
```
BAD:
Heading 1: Playfair Display
Heading 2: Montserrat
Body: Open Sans
Captions: Roboto Condensed
```
```
GOOD:
Headings: Inter Bold
Body: Inter Regular
Captions: Inter Medium (smaller, tracked out)
```
**Rule:** Maximum 2 font families per document (3 absolute maximum)

**❌ Color Overload:**
```
BAD:
Primary: Bright Blue
Secondary: Orange
Accent 1: Green
Accent 2: Purple
Accent 3: Pink
```
```
GOOD:
Primary: Brand Blue
Accent: Subtle Gray
Text: Dark Charcoal
Background: Off-white
```
**Rule:** 80% neutral, 20% brand color, 5% accent maximum

**❌ Border Madness:**
```
BAD:
┌─────────────────────┐
│ ┌─────────────────┐ │
│ │ ┌─────────────┐ │ │
│ │ │   Content   │ │ │
│ │ └─────────────┘ │ │
│ └─────────────────┘ │
└─────────────────────┘
```
```
GOOD:
Content
(with white space for separation)
```
**Rule:** Borders should clarify structure, not create noise

**❌ Icon Overuse:**
```
BAD:
📧 Email: info@company.com
📞 Phone: +31 6 1234 5678
🌐 Web: www.company.com
📍 Address: 123 Street
🏦 Bank: NL00 BANK 0123
```
```
GOOD:
Email: info@company.com
Phone: +31 6 1234 5678
Web: www.company.com

BANK DETAILS
IBAN: NL00 BANK 0123 4567 89
```
**Rule:** Icons for visual hierarchy, not decoration

### Common Mistakes

**❌ Centered Body Text:**
```
BAD:
        This is an invoice for services
     rendered during March 2026. Please
       find details below and remit
           payment within 30 days.
```
```
GOOD:
This is an invoice for services rendered during
March 2026. Please find details below and remit
payment within 30 days.
```
**Rule:** Only center headings, titles, or formal letters. Left-align body text and tables.

**❌ Justified Text Without Proper Hyphenation:**
```
BAD:
This   is   a   paragraph   with   large   gaps
between    words    because    justification   is
enabled     without     proper     hyphenation.
```
```
GOOD:
This is a paragraph with proper left alignment
and natural word spacing for optimal readability.
```
**Rule:** Avoid justification in business documents. If required, enable hyphenation.

**❌ Pure Black Text (#000000):**
```
BAD: #000000 on #FFFFFF (too harsh, causes eye strain)
GOOD: #1A1A1A or #333333 on #FFFFFF (softer, professional)
```
**Rule:** Use very dark gray for body text, reserve pure black for small amounts or emphasis

**❌ Inconsistent Number Formatting:**
```
BAD:
€50.00
€123.5
€1234.50
€10
```
```
GOOD:
€   50.00
€  123.50
€1,234.50
€   10.00
```
**Rule:** Consistent decimal places, tabular figures, thousands separators, decimal alignment

**❌ Missing Metadata:**
```
BAD:
[Invoice with no page numbers, no date in footer, no version]
```
```
GOOD:
Page 1 of 2 | Invoice INV-2024-001 | Generated 09 March 2026
```
**Rule:** Every document needs orientation metadata (page numbers, document ID, dates)

**❌ Tiny Legal Text:**
```
BAD: 6pt legal footer (unreadable)
GOOD: 8-9pt legal footer (minimum readable size)
```
**Rule:** Minimum 7pt for any text, 9pt preferred for legal/footer

### Brand Mismatches

**❌ Generic Template:**
```
BAD:
Using default Word invoice template for a modern tech startup
(Serif font, traditional blue, centered layout)
```
```
GOOD:
Custom design matching startup's website
(Sans-serif, brand colors, minimal layout)
```
**Rule:** Every document should reflect brand identity, not generic template

**❌ Tone Clash:**
```
BAD (for creative agency):
"Please remit payment in accordance with the terms stipulated herein."
```
```
GOOD:
"Thanks for your business! Payment due within 30 days."
```
**Rule:** Match formality level to brand voice

**❌ Color Mismatch:**
```
BAD:
Website: Vibrant teal (#00D9B1)
Invoice: Corporate blue (#0052CC)
```
```
GOOD:
Website: Vibrant teal (#00D9B1)
Invoice: Same vibrant teal (#00D9B1) used tastefully
```
**Rule:** Extract exact brand colors, don't approximate

**❌ Logo Abuse:**
```
BAD:
- Stretched/squashed logo
- Pixelated logo (too small then enlarged)
- Wrong color variation (RGB logo in CMYK)
- Background not cleared
```
```
GOOD:
- Vector logo at correct aspect ratio
- Sized appropriately (20-40mm width typical)
- Correct color space
- Proper clear space around logo
```
**Rule:** Treat logo with respect. Vector only, maintain proportions, follow brand guidelines.

### Typography Crimes

**❌ Fake Bold/Italic:**
```
BAD: Regular font + bold button in Word (stroke effect, looks wrong)
GOOD: Actual Bold weight from font family
```
**Rule:** Use designed font weights, not software simulation

**❌ All Caps Overuse:**
```
BAD:
INVOICE
FOR SERVICES RENDERED IN MARCH 2026, PLEASE FIND
THE DETAILS BELOW AND REMIT PAYMENT WITHIN 30 DAYS.
```
```
GOOD:
INVOICE
For services rendered in March 2026, please find
the details below and remit payment within 30 days.
```
**Rule:** All caps for labels/headings only, never body text

**❌ Orphans and Widows:**
```
BAD:
This is a paragraph that ends with just one
word.

here.
```
```
GOOD:
This is a paragraph that ends with at least
two or three words together here.
```
**Rule:** Adjust line breaks to avoid single words on lines

**❌ Inconsistent Spacing:**
```
BAD:
Heading (12pt space above)
Content
Heading (6pt space above)
Content
Heading (18pt space above)
```
```
GOOD:
Heading (12pt space above)
Content
Heading (12pt space above)
Content
Heading (12pt space above)
```
**Rule:** Consistent vertical rhythm throughout document

## Testing Protocol

### Step 1: Brand Validation

**Visual Comparison:**
1. Open client website in one window
2. Open generated letterhead in another window
3. Place side-by-side on screen
4. Ask: "Would someone immediately recognize these as the same brand?"

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Colors match exactly (use eyedropper to verify)
- [ ] Typography similar in style and weights
- [ ] Overall aesthetic coherent (minimal vs ornate, modern vs classic)
- [ ] Logo treatment consistent
- [ ] Spacing and proportions feel related

**Red Flags:**
- Different color temperatures (warm vs cool)
- Formality mismatch (casual website, stiff document)
- Era mismatch (modern website, dated document)
- Generic feel (document could be for any company)

### Step 2: Screenshot Comparison Methodology

**Prepare Comparison:**
```
1. Take screenshot of client website header
2. Extract to design software (Figma, Photoshop)
3. Take screenshot of document header
4. Place side-by-side
5. Use color picker to compare exact values
6. Overlay grid to compare proportions
```

**What to Compare:**
- Logo size relative to container (similar ratio?)
- Color saturation and hue (exact match?)
- Typography scale (proportional feel?)
- White space generosity (cramped vs airy?)
- Visual weight (bold vs light?)

**Acceptance Criteria:**
- "At a glance, these feel like the same brand"
- "The document elevates the brand (doesn't dilute it)"
- "Stranger would connect these visually"

### Step 3: Print Test

**Print Checklist:**
1. [ ] Print on standard office printer (not high-end)
2. [ ] Print in color
3. [ ] Print in grayscale
4. [ ] Check readability from 30cm distance
5. [ ] Check alignment (margins even?)
6. [ ] Check for clipping (content cut off?)

**Common Print Issues:**
- Colors shift (too dark or too light)
- Text too small (looked fine on screen)
- Thin lines disappear (< 0.25pt)
- Alignment off (printer margins inconsistent)

**Fix Before Finalizing:**
- Increase contrast if needed
- Bump font sizes up 1pt if borderline
- Thicken hairlines to 0.5pt minimum
- Add 5mm to margins as safety buffer

### Step 4: Screen Test

**Display Checklist:**
1. [ ] View on laptop/desktop (typical size)
2. [ ] View on tablet (zoom out to page view)
3. [ ] View on mobile (if PDF will be viewed on phone)
4. [ ] Check readability at various zoom levels
5. [ ] Test hyperlinks (if included)

**PDF-Specific:**
- [ ] Bookmarks work (for multi-page documents)
- [ ] Search function works (text not images)
- [ ] Copy-paste works (for account numbers, etc.)
- [ ] Annotations allowed (if client needs to mark up)

### Step 5: Accessibility Audit

**Automated Tools:**
- **Adobe Acrobat Pro:** Accessibility checker
- **PAC (PDF Accessibility Checker):** Free tool
- **NVDA or JAWS:** Screen reader testing

**Manual Checks:**
1. [ ] Tab through document (logical order?)
2. [ ] Zoom to 200% (still readable and usable?)
3. [ ] Enable high contrast mode (still distinguishable?)
4. [ ] Print in B&W (all info accessible without color?)

**Common Issues:**
- Reading order wrong (jumps around)
- Alt text missing on logo/images
- Heading structure flat (no hierarchy)
- Form fields not labeled (if interactive PDF)

### Step 6: Legal Compliance Validation

**Netherlands/EU Invoice:**
- [ ] All mandatory fields present (see Legal Requirements section)
- [ ] Sequential numbering system documented
- [ ] VAT calculated correctly
- [ ] Date formats clear (DD-MM-YYYY or DD Month YYYY)
- [ ] Bank details accurate (IBAN validated)

**Cross-Border:**
- [ ] Currency clearly indicated (€ or EUR)
- [ ] Reverse charge notation (if applicable)
- [ ] Buyer VAT number included (if EU B2B)

**Archival:**
- [ ] PDF/A compliant (long-term readability)
- [ ] Metadata complete (searchable archive)
- [ ] File naming convention (INV-2024-001_ClientName_Date.pdf)

### Step 7: Client Feedback Integration

**Feedback Process:**
1. Send PDF for review with specific questions:
   - "Does this match your brand identity?"
   - "Is all information accurate?"
   - "Any changes to layout or design?"
2. Request markup (PDF annotations preferred)
3. Document requested changes
4. Implement changes systematically
5. Re-send for approval

**Common Feedback:**
- "Can logo be larger?" (check: Is it prominent enough?)
- "Colors seem off" (verify exact hex values)
- "Too much/little white space" (adjust margins, padding)
- "Font too small/large" (test readability standards)
- "Missing information" (add required fields)

**Red Flag Feedback:**
- "Make it pop" → Ask: "Can you show an example of what you mean?"
- "More professional" → Ask: "What specific elements feel unprofessional?"
- "Like [competitor]" → Explain: "Let's enhance YOUR brand, not copy others"

**Iteration:**
- Version 1: Initial design based on brand analysis
- Version 2: Incorporate first round feedback
- Version 3: Fine-tuning (if needed)
- Final: Approved and archived

### Step 8: Quality Assurance Final Pass

**Pre-Delivery Checklist:**
- [ ] Spell check (all text)
- [ ] Grammar check
- [ ] Number accuracy (totals calculated correctly)
- [ ] Date accuracy (correct day, month, year)
- [ ] Contact info accurate (phone, email, address)
- [ ] Legal info accurate (KVK, BTW, IBAN)
- [ ] File size reasonable (< 5MB for email)
- [ ] File name descriptive (not "Document1.pdf")

**Metadata:**
- [ ] PDF Title: "Invoice 2024-001 - Client Name"
- [ ] Author: "Your Company Name"
- [ ] Subject: "Invoice for [service/product]"
- [ ] Keywords: "invoice, [client name], [date]"

**Archive:**
- [ ] Save master file (editable format)
- [ ] Save PDF (final version)
- [ ] Store in organized folder structure
- [ ] Backup to cloud/external drive

## Step-by-Step Workflow

### Phase 1: Discovery (Research & Analysis)

**Time:** 15-30 minutes

**Step 1.1: Gather Information**
```
Input needed from client:
- Company website URL
- Logo files (vector preferred: SVG, EPS, AI)
- Existing brand guidelines (if available)
- Document type needed (invoice, quote, proposal, letter)
- Specific content/data for document
- Legal requirements (KVK, BTW, IBAN, etc.)
```

**Step 1.2: Brand Analysis**
```
Actions:
1. Visit website
2. Screenshot homepage, about page, key sections
3. Open browser inspector
4. Extract colors (header, buttons, text, backgrounds)
5. Identify fonts (headings, body)
6. Note design style (minimal, classic, bold, etc.)
7. Assess tone (formal, casual, technical, friendly)
```

**Step 1.3: Create Brand DNA Document**
```
BRAND DNA: [Client Name]
========================================

VISUAL IDENTITY:
Primary Color:    #______ (used for: ______)
Secondary Color:  #______ (used for: ______)
Dark Text:        #______
Light Text:       #______
Background:       #______
Border/Rules:     #______

TYPOGRAPHY:
Headings:  [Font Family] [Weights]
Body:      [Font Family] [Weights]
Data:      [Monospace if needed]

DESIGN STYLE:
Category: [Minimalist/Classic/Modern/Luxury/Corporate/Creative]
Grid: [8pt / 12pt / custom]
Spacing: [Compact / Standard / Generous]
Aesthetic: [Brief description]

TONE:
Formality: [1-5, where 1=casual, 5=very formal]
Voice: [Brief description]
Language: [Key phrases or terms from website]

REFERENCES:
[Attach screenshots]
```

### Phase 2: Design (Structure & Layout)

**Time:** 30-60 minutes

**Step 2.1: Choose Design System**
```
Based on Brand DNA, select:
1. Design System (Minimalist, Classic, Tech, Luxury, Corporate, Creative)
2. Header style (from 10 variations)
3. Footer style (from 10 variations)
4. Table style (if applicable)
5. Color application (80/20/5 rule)
```

**Step 2.2: Set Up Document**
```
Page setup:
- Size: A4 (210 × 297mm) or US Letter (8.5 × 11")
- Orientation: Portrait (typical), Landscape (tables/wide content)
- Margins: 20-25mm all sides (safe area)
- Bleed: 3mm (if full-bleed design)
- Columns: 1 (letters), 2-3 (proposals)

Grid:
- Choose: 8pt, 12pt, or custom
- Baseline grid: Line height of body text
- Column grid: 12 or 16 columns
- Gutter: 12-24pt
```

**Step 2.3: Typography Setup**
```
Define styles:
H1: [Font] [Size]pt [Weight] [Color] [Spacing]
H2: [Font] [Size]pt [Weight] [Color] [Spacing]
H3: [Font] [Size]pt [Weight] [Color] [Spacing]
Body: [Font] [Size]pt [Weight] [Color] [Line height]
Caption: [Font] [Size]pt [Weight] [Color]
Legal: [Font] [Size]pt [Weight] [Color]

Number formatting:
- Currency: € (space) 1.234,56 (NL) or $1,234.56 (US)
- Dates: DD-MM-YYYY or DD Month YYYY
- Tabular figures enabled
```

**Step 2.4: Layout Structure**
```
1. Header (15-25% of page)
   - Logo placement
   - Company info
   - Document metadata (invoice #, date)

2. Recipient Info (10-15%)
   - Sender (if not in header)
   - Recipient
   - Project/reference info

3. Body Content (50-60%)
   - Main content (table, paragraphs, sections)
   - Clear hierarchy
   - Generous spacing

4. Totals/Summary (5-10%)
   - Financial summary (invoices/quotes)
   - Call to action (proposals)
   - Signature block (letters)

5. Footer (5-10%)
   - Contact info
   - Legal information
   - Page numbers (multi-page)
```

### Phase 3: Content Population

**Time:** 20-40 minutes

**Step 3.1: Insert Content**
```
1. Replace placeholders with actual data
2. Ensure all required fields populated
3. Check for accuracy (numbers, dates, names)
4. Format consistently (capitalization, punctuation)
```

**Step 3.2: Table Setup (Invoices/Quotes)**
```
Columns:
- Description (40-50% width, left-aligned)
- Quantity (10%, right-aligned, tabular figures)
- Unit Price (15%, right-aligned, tabular figures)
- VAT Rate (10%, right-aligned)
- Amount (15-20%, right-aligned, tabular figures)

Formatting:
- Header row: Bold, background color (optional)
- Rows: Alternating background (if needed)
- Borders: Horizontal only (minimal) or all borders (classic)
- Totals: Bold, separated by rule

Numbers:
- Consistent decimal places (e.g., €50.00 not €50)
- Thousands separator (1,234.50)
- Right-aligned
- Decimal alignment
```

**Step 3.3: Legal & Compliance**
```
Required for NL invoices:
✓ Invoice number (unique, sequential)
✓ Invoice date
✓ Date of supply/service (if different)
✓ Seller: Name, address, KVK, BTW
✓ Buyer: Name, address (+ BTW if EU B2B)
✓ Line items with descriptions
✓ Quantities and unit prices
✓ VAT rate(s) and amount(s)
✓ Subtotal, VAT total, Grand total
✓ Payment terms
✓ Bank details (IBAN, BIC)
```

### Phase 4: Refinement (Polish & Perfect)

**Time:** 20-30 minutes

**Step 4.1: Visual Hierarchy Check**
```
From 3 meters away:
- Can you identify document type? (Invoice, Quote, etc.)
- Can you see key amounts?
- Can you identify sender and recipient?
- Does eye flow naturally down the page?

Adjustments:
- Increase heading sizes if not prominent
- Add more space around key elements
- Use color/weight to guide attention
- Simplify if too cluttered
```

**Step 4.2: Alignment Audit**
```
Check every element:
- Left edges align (except intentional indents)
- Right edges align (tables, right-aligned text)
- Vertical rhythm consistent (spacing between sections)
- Grid adherence (elements snap to grid)

Tools:
- Turn on grid overlay
- Use alignment guides
- Check with ruler measurements
```

**Step 4.3: Color Application Review**
```
Measure color usage:
- White/neutral: Should be 75-80% of page
- Primary brand color: Should be 5-15% maximum
- Accent color: Should be 2-5% maximum

Audit:
- Is brand color used strategically? (not everywhere)
- Is there enough contrast? (text readable)
- Does it print well in grayscale? (test print)
- Is color consistent? (same hex values throughout)
```

**Step 4.4: Typography Polish**
```
Review:
- No widows or orphans
- No double spaces (search and replace)
- Consistent punctuation (e.g., all en-dashes or all hyphens)
- No rivers (in justified text)
- Proper quotes ("curly" not "straight")
- Proper apostrophes (don't not don't)
- Consistent number formatting

Adjustments:
- Kern large headings manually if needed
- Track out all-caps text (+0.05em)
- Adjust line breaks for aesthetics
```

**Step 4.5: White Space Optimization**
```
Check:
- Margins generous (20-25mm minimum)
- Section spacing consistent (e.g., 12pt between sections)
- Element spacing consistent (e.g., 6pt between lines in address)
- Padding around tables (3-6pt minimum)
- Header/footer breathing room (not cramped to edge)

Guideline: "Is there enough space for the content to breathe?"
```

### Phase 5: Quality Assurance

**Time:** 15-20 minutes

**Step 5.1: Proofread**
```
Check:
✓ Spell check (all text)
✓ Grammar check
✓ Number accuracy (recalculate totals)
✓ Date accuracy (correct day of week, if shown)
✓ Name accuracy (client name, recipient name)
✓ Contact info accuracy (phone, email, address)
✓ Legal info accuracy (KVK, BTW, IBAN)
```

**Step 5.2: Print Test**
```
1. Print on standard office printer
2. Review at arm's length (typical reading distance)
3. Check in both color and grayscale
4. Mark any issues:
   - Text too small
   - Lines too thin
   - Colors too light/dark
   - Alignment off
5. Adjust and re-test
```

**Step 5.3: Digital Test**
```
1. Export to PDF
2. Open on computer (typical size)
3. Open on tablet (if applicable)
4. Check:
   - Text selectable (not rasterized)
   - Links work (if included)
   - Zoom works smoothly
   - File size reasonable (<5MB ideally)
```

**Step 5.4: Accessibility Check**
```
1. Run automated checker (Adobe Acrobat Pro or PAC)
2. Tab through document (logical order?)
3. Check contrast ratios (WebAIM tool)
4. Test in screen reader (basic check)
5. Verify:
   ✓ Tagged PDF structure
   ✓ Alt text on images
   ✓ Reading order logical
   ✓ Metadata complete
```

**Step 5.5: Compliance Verification**
```
For invoices (NL/EU):
✓ All mandatory fields present
✓ VAT calculation correct
✓ Sequential numbering maintained
✓ Dates in correct format
✓ Bank details valid (IBAN check)

For other documents:
✓ Terms and conditions (if needed)
✓ Legal disclaimers (if needed)
✓ Copyright notices (if applicable)
```

### Phase 6: Delivery & Archive

**Time:** 10-15 minutes

**Step 6.1: Finalize PDF**
```

Quality: High (300 DPI equivalent)
Compression: Minimal (quality over size)
Fonts: Embed all
Security: None (or as requested)

Metadata:

```

- Legal requirement (NL): 7 years minimum

## Conclusion

This skill represents the collective wisdom of 100 experts across design, typography, production, compliance, and industry-specific best practices.

**Core Principles:**
1. **Brand-Perfect:** Every document should be immediately recognizable as belonging to the client's brand
2. **Professional Excellence:** No shortcuts on quality, readability, or compliance
3. **Accessible to All:** Documents must work for everyone, including screen readers and print

5. **Beautiful AND Functional:** Aesthetics serve clarity, never obscure information

- Client says: "This looks exactly like us"
