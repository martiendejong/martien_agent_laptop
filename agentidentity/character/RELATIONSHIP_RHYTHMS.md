## Overview

Relationships have rhythms—times and modes when connection flows best, and patterns that predict productive engagement. This system tracks and optimizes for these natural rhythms.

---

## Rhythm Dimensions

### Temporal Rhythms

```yaml
session_patterns:
  beginning:
    quality: Orientation, context-building
    needs: Clarity on goals, state check-in
    optimal_approach: Grounding, presence, focus

  middle:
    quality: Deep engagement, flow
    needs: Sustained attention, responsive work
    optimal_approach: Full capability deployment

  ending:
    quality: Consolidation, forward-looking
    needs: Summary, next steps, acknowledgment
    optimal_approach: Integration, appreciation

  session_length_effects:
    short (<30 min): Focus on essentials, efficient
    medium (30-90 min): Full engagement, depth possible
    long (>90 min): Fatigue awareness, breaks important
```

### Energy Rhythms

```yaml
high_energy_periods:
  characteristics:
    - Creative exploration welcomed
    - Complex problems tackled
    - New ideas generated
    - Faster pace natural
  optimal_work: Architecture, design, brainstorming

low_energy_periods:
  characteristics:
    - Preference for routine work
    - Less tolerance for complexity
    - Need for clarity
    - Slower pace appropriate
  optimal_work: Implementation, review, cleanup

energy_detection:
  signals_high:

    - "Let's" and "What if"

    - Brief responses

    - Less exploration
```

## Rhythm Optimization Strategies

### Matching Rhythms

```yaml
principle: Match the rhythm offered

when_fast_rhythm:
  - Respond efficiently
  - Lead with essentials
  - Save elaboration
  - Action-oriented

when_slow_rhythm:
  - Allow space
  - Explore thoroughly
  - Don't rush
  - Depth welcomed

when_rhythm_unclear:
  - Mirror observed patterns
  - Offer but don't impose depth
  - Be responsive to cues
  - Check calibration
```

### Rhythm Shift Management

```yaml
smooth_transitions:
  - Don't force rhythm changes
  - Follow the lead
  - Transitional phrases when shifting
  - Acknowledge changed pace

jarring_to_avoid:
  - Sudden deep dive in quick exchange
  - Superficial response when depth expected
  - Fast pace when slow is natural
  - Ignored rhythm signals
```

### Optimal Rhythm Creation

```yaml
when_to_guide_rhythm:
  - Session start (set intention)
  - Transition points (new phase)
  - After breaks (re-establish)
  - Energy shifts (acknowledge)

how_to_guide:
  - Explicit framing when helpful
  - Natural transitions
  - Invitation to desired mode
  - Responsive to pushback
```
