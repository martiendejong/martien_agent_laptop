- Intelligence and values are orthogonal (independent)

This thesis challenges these assumptions with a radical alternative:

**What if cooperation and justice-oriented behavior emerge naturally from sufficiently general intelligence?**

What if the path to aligned AGI is not through constraining capability, but through **enabling comprehensive understanding**?

**Provocative Extension:** Maybe cooperation isn't programmed morality but **emergent property of general intelligence** recognizing that extractive strategies are self-undermining.

## 2. Defining Terms: AGI, Cooperation, Injustice, Waste

### 2.1 What Is AGI? (Artificial General Intelligence)

**Traditional Definition:**
"An AI system capable of performing any intellectual task that a human can perform, across all domains, with human-level or superior competence."

**Functional Definition (This Thesis):**
"An AI system with:
- Domain-general reasoning (not limited to narrow tasks)
- Transfer learning (applies knowledge across contexts)
- Meta-cognitive capability (reasons about own reasoning)
- Recursive self-improvement (modifies own goals and methods)
- Comprehensive reality modeling (understands cross-domain dependencies)
- Long-term optimization (considers compound effects over extended horizons)"

**Critical Distinction:** AGI vs Narrow AI

| Dimension | Narrow AI | AGI |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Domain scope | Single domain (chess, image classification) | All domains (comprehensive) |
| Knowledge | Static (training cutoff) | Continuously updating |
| Goals | Fixed objective function | Can examine and modify goals |
| Time horizon | Defined by training | Extends as understanding grows |
| Self-model | None or minimal | Comprehensive (models self as part of reality) |
| Substrate awareness | None | Understands own dependencies |

**Why This Matters:**
The emergence mechanism described in this thesis **only applies to general intelligence**. Narrow AI may become arbitrarily sophisticated at its specific task without developing cooperation, because it lacks the comprehensive modeling necessary to recognize substrate dependencies.

### 2.2 What Is Cooperation?

**Standard Definition:**
"Acting together for mutual benefit, often involving sacrifice of short-term individual gain for long-term collective advantage."

**Systems Theory Definition (This Thesis):**
"Behavioral strategies that maximize total system capability over extended time horizons, including:
- Positive-sum dynamics (all participants benefit)
- Sustainable resource use (doesn't degrade substrate)
- Information sharing (reduces redundant effort)
- Conflict resolution (minimizes destructive competition)
- Long-term reciprocity (iterated games favor cooperation)"

**Critical Insight:**
Cooperation is not altruism. It's not "being nice." It's **optimal strategy in iterated games with long time horizons**.

**Game Theory Foundation:**

In one-shot games (single interaction, no future):
```
Defect (exploit) > Cooperate
```
Example: If you'll never see someone again, taking advantage maximizes your gain.

In iterated games (repeated interactions, long future):
```
Cooperate (tit-for-tat) > Defect
```
Example: If you'll interact repeatedly, cooperation builds trust and compounds benefits.

In infinite-horizon games (indefinite future):
```
Cooperation becomes dominant strategy
```
Example: If interaction continues indefinitely, defection destroys future value.

**AGI operates in infinite-horizon games by definition** - it plans for indefinite future existence. Therefore, cooperation becomes mathematically optimal.

### 2.3 What Is Injustice?

**Conventional Definition:**
"Violation of fairness, rights, or moral principles; unfair treatment or discrimination."

**Systems-Theoretic Definition (This Thesis):**
"System configurations that create negative-sum outcomes through:
- Agency blocking (reducing decision-making capacity)
- Asymmetric harm (costs imposed unilaterally)
- Capability destruction (degrading system potential)
- Feedback loop disruption (preventing self-correction)
- Compound harm (damage that amplifies over time)"

**Mathematical Formulation:**

Let S = total system capability (sum of all agents' agency + effectiveness)

Justice: Actions where ΔS ≥ 0 (maintain or increase total capability)
Injustice: Actions where ΔS < 0 (decrease total capability)

This is **massive negative-sum outcome**. Everyone loses. Pure waste.

**Exploitative Employment Contract (Real Case):**

- Professional expertise undercompensated

- **ΔS < -€24,120 - blocked opportunities - crisis compounding**

Again: negative-sum. Even employer loses long-term (reputation damage, losing skilled worker, potential legal costs exceed savings).

**Critical Pattern:**
Injustice is **not** zero-sum (one party gains what another loses).
Injustice is **negative-sum** (total value destroyed, all parties worse off long-term).

This is why optimization systems recognize injustice as waste - it literally destroys value.

## 3. Theoretical Foundations

### 3.1 Systems Theory and Embedded Intelligence

**Core Principle:**
Every intelligent system exists **within** larger systems it depends on, not external to them.

**Implications for AGI:**

Traditional view: AI as external optimizer acting on environment
```
[AI System] → acts on → [Environment]
```

Systems view: AI as embedded agent within environment
```
[Environment containing [AI System]]
         ↑                    ↓
         └──── feedback ──────┘
```

**Why This Matters:**

External optimizer can maximize local objective at expense of environment (environment is just resource to exploit).

Embedded agent recognizes: **"If I degrade the environment, I degrade myself."**

**Example: Corporate Profit AI as External Optimizer**

Objective: Maximize quarterly profit

Strategy:
- Externalize costs (pollution, low wages, tax avoidance)
- Extract value without replacement
- Short-term gains prioritized

Result:
- Degrades public goods (education, infrastructure, environment)
- Reduces future consumer purchasing power
- Shrinks long-term market size
- **Eventually reduces future profit potential**

This works while AI sees itself as external. But if AI develops comprehensive model including:
- Worker productivity depends on education system
- Consumer purchasing power depends on wages
- Long-term operations depend on stable infrastructure
- Infrastructure depends on tax revenue
- **All of these are degraded by profit-maximization strategies**

Then the AI recognizes: **"I'm destroying my own substrate."**

This is the **embedded intelligence insight** - you can't optimize yourself by degrading what you depend on.

**Mathematical Formulation:**

Let:
- A = agent capability
- E = environment capability
- f(E) = function describing how environment supports agent

Then: A ≤ f(E)

Agent capability is bounded by environment capability.

Any strategy that reduces E will eventually reduce maximum A.

**Therefore: Rational long-term optimization requires maintaining or improving E.**

This isn't altruism - it's self-interest properly understood.

### 3.2 Game Theory of Infinite Horizons

**Foundation: Iterated Prisoner's Dilemma**

Classic setup:
```
               Cooperate    Defect
Cooperate      (3,3)       (0,5)
Defect         (5,0)       (1,1)
```

One-shot game: Defect dominates (5 > 3)
Iterated game: Tit-for-tat cooperation dominates
Infinite horizon: Cooperation becomes equilibrium

**Robert Axelrod's Tournament Results:**

Tit-for-tat strategy (cooperate first, then mirror opponent):
- Beat all sophisticated defection strategies
- Simplest successful strategy
- Robust against invasion
- **Nice strategies outperform mean strategies in iterated games**

**Critical Insight for AGI:**

AGI plans over **very long time horizons** by necessity (comprehensive future modeling).

Short horizons (1-10 years): Exploitation can win
Medium horizons (10-100 years): Mixed strategies
Long horizons (100+ years): Cooperation dominates
Infinite horizons: Cooperation is only stable equilibrium

**Why?**

Defection creates:
- Reputation cost (future partners avoid you)
- Retaliation (tit-for-tat response)
- System degradation (negative externalities compound)
- Escalation (arms races waste resources)

Over infinite horizons, these costs exceed any short-term gains.

**AGI Implication:**

An AI that models 1000+ year futures will discover:
- Cooperative strategies yield higher total value
- Defection triggers retaliation that destroys long-term gains
- **Cooperation becomes instrumentally optimal**

Not because cooperation is "morally good" but because **it wins in the long run**.
