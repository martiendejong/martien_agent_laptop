### Philosophical Framework: "Prosperity Is a System"
(Blog post by Martien, Dec 30, 2025)

**Seven Core Principles:**

1. **Isolated Innovation Underperforms**
   "Technical quality alone does not determine success. Context, trust, ownership, and feedback loops matter just as much."

2. **Prosperity as Emergent Property**
   Sustainable prosperity requires three foundations:
   - Stability
   - Human agency
   - Adaptive learning capacity

   (Not just GDP metrics)

3. **Extractive Technology (Anti-pattern)**
   Systems become harmful when they:
   - Remove decision-making from affected communities
   - Prioritize short-term gains over resilience
   - Concentrate benefits while dispersing risks

4. **Human-in-the-Loop as Safety**
   Maintaining people within critical decision points:
   - Preserves accountability
   - Prevents silent failures
   - This is **structural safety**, not inefficiency

5. **Institutional Memory Matters**
   Organizations lacking designed memory systems:
   - Repeat mistakes
   - Lose learning when personnel changes
   - Fail to compound knowledge

6. **Prospergenics Design Framework**
   Four principles guide prosperity-oriented design:
   - **Human agency first** (before optimization)
   - **Feedback before optimization** (measure what matters)
   - **Memory by design** (systems that learn)
   - **Long-term alignment over extraction** (sustainable not extractive)

7. **Urgency Now**
   As AI scales rapidly, designing systems that "deserve their power" becomes increasingly critical.

**Conclusion:** Sustainable prosperity requires cultivating capability within communities rather than creating dependency on external solutions.

#### Pastoralist Economics Research
**"Goat Trading as a Maasai Investment Model"** (Timothy Opiyo, Dec 21, 2025)

**Economic Model:**
Structured goat trading as scalable investment combining financial returns with climate resilience

**Investment Cycle:**
- **Purchase:** KES 3,000–5,000 per goat
- **Care costs:** KES 1,000–2,000 over 3–6 months
- **Sale price:** KES 7,000–12,000 or higher

**Climate Resilience Benefits:**
- Goats require less water than other livestock
- Survive on sparse vegetation
- Function as convertible emergency assets during droughts

**Four-Stage Trading Model:**
1. **Sourcing** - Healthy animals from markets and pastoral households
2. **Fattening** - Supplementary feeding and veterinary care
3. **Timing** - Sales around demand spikes and seasonal markets
4. **Reinvesting** - Profits into larger herds or improved breeds

**Social Impact:**
- Women and youth participation opportunities
- Informal savings groups formation
- Livestock health skill development
- Household food security improvements

**Risk Management:**
- Preventive veterinary practices
- Collective selling strategies
- Herd diversification

## 5. 💰 Maasai Investments - Wholesome Microinvestments

### Identity
**Website:** https://maasaiinvestments.com
**Tagline:** "Wholesome Investing Empowers People"

### Problem Statement
**Many families across Africa have:**
- Skills and will to work
- **But lack the means of production**

**Game-changers:**
- A goat ($35)
- A sewing machine
- A bag of seeds ($10)
- A few fruit trees ($5 each)

**Result:** Difference between poverty and prosperity

### Solution
**Simple, open-source initiative** connecting:
- Small entrepreneurs
- Local ambassadors
- Microinvestors

**Characteristics:**
- Quick, transparent, easy to set up
- Methods publicly available
- Anyone can start in their community

### Three Required Roles

#### 1. Entrepreneur
- Someone with drive to work
- Skills in: herding animals, growing crops, producing goods
- Knows their craft but needs resources to begin

#### 2. Ambassador
- Trusted local contact
- Access to potential microinvestors
- Explains concept, gathers investments
- Manages communication between investors and entrepreneur

#### 3. Investors
- People willing to contribute small amounts
- Money directly converts into production means
- Each contribution linked to specific item ("you own this goat")

### Investment Examples
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Fruit tree | $5 |
| Seeds | $10 |
| Cloth | $25 |
| Goat | $35 |

### How It Works

#### Step 1: Collect Investments
- Ambassador reaches out to investors
- Each contribution linked directly to production item
- Transparency: "you own this goat" or "you funded these seeds"

#### Step 2: Transfer Funds
- Once pledged, ambassador forwards money to entrepreneur
- Entrepreneur acquires agreed production means

#### Step 3: Produce and Sell
- Entrepreneur uses resource to generate income
  - Milk from goats
  - Crops from seeds
  - Fabric from cloth
  - Fruit from trees

#### Step 4: Profit Sharing
After deducting necessary expenses, profits split fairly in **three equal parts:**

- **1/3 for investor** - Return on microinvestment
- **1/3 for ambassador** - For managing the process
- **1/3 for entrepreneur** - For their labor and risk

### Example: Goat Investment

**Initial Costs:**
- Goat purchase: $35
- Fees: $2
- Insurance: $6
- Feeding and care: $25
- **Total: $68**

**After One Year:**
- Selling price: $70
- **Profit: $12**

**Split Three Ways:**
- Investor: **$4**
- Ambassador: **$4**
- Entrepreneur: **$4**

**Scale to 100 Goats:**
Each party earns: **$400**
- Decent return for investor
- Family gains steady income

### Tools Provided

**Ready-to-use resources:**
1. **Spreadsheets** - Bookkeeping and profit tracking
2. **Prewritten contracts** - Formalize agreement between parties
3. **Ownership certificates** - For investors
4. **Website (in development)** - For easier access

### Implementation Guide

**Start Your Own Maasai Investment Circle:**

1. Identify motivated entrepreneur in your community
2. Find ambassador who can connect with microinvestors
3. Use spreadsheets, contracts, and templates
4. **Start small:** One goat, one tree, one piece of cloth
5. Track, report, and share results transparently
6. **Scale gradually** as trust builds

### Why It Works

**Direct impact:**
Every dollar goes straight into production

**Low risk:**
- Investments small
- Tied to real goods
- Insured

**Shared success:**
All parties benefit equally

**Scalable:**
Works whether you start with one goat or hundred

### Philosophy

**Not a charity** - Sustainable, local business model where **everyone wins:**
- Families gain means to work
- Ambassadors earn for coordination
- Investors receive fair return while making real difference

### Open Source Commitment

Methods are **open-source**. Anyone can begin today.

**Contact:** https://martiendejong.nl/contact/ for:
