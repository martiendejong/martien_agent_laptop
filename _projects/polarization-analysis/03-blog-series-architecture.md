## PART 5: WRITING GUIDELINES

### Tone & Voice

**DO:**
- Be conversational (person-to-person, not lecture)
- Use "we" (we're in this together)
- Show vulnerability (I'm worried too, I've been in bubbles too)
- Be specific (examples, data, stories)
- Use humor (where appropriate - mockery of grifters, self-deprecation)
- Vary pace (short punchy sentences + longer explanatory)

**DON'T:**
- Be academic/jargony (this is public, not peer review)
- Both-sides to death (acknowledge nuance but have a view)
- Shame readers (meet them where they are)
- Be preachy (show, don't moralize)
- Overuse qualifiers (not every sentence needs "maybe" or "I think")

---

### Structure

**Every post follows:**
1. **Hook** (first 100 words grab them)
2. **Promise** (here's what you'll learn)
3. **Deliver** (content with sub-headers, visual breaks)
4. **Callback** (reference earlier posts, build narrative continuity)
5. **CTA** (what to do next)

**Pacing:**
- New insight every 200-300 words (don't let reader get bored)
- Visual break every 400-500 words (image, diagram, pull quote)
- Emotional beat every 500-700 words (make them feel something)

---

### Credibility Signals

**Include:**
- Data (but not overwhelming - key stats only)
- Expert consensus (the 1000-expert panel framing)
- Historical precedents (this happened before)
- Current examples (recognizable, recent)
- Acknowledging uncertainty (where we don't know, say so)

**Avoid:**
- Overclaiming (be ambitious but realistic)
- Conspiracy thinking (even when criticizing)
- Ad hominem (attack ideas, not people)
- Tribalism (we're trying to END that)

**Pivot triggers:**
