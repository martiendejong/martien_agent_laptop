# You're Not Crazy, The System Is

**Part 1 of 12: The Narcissism Pandemic Series**

---

You've felt it, right?

That creeping sense that everyone has lost their minds. Your uncle shares conspiracy theories that sound like they're from a parallel universe. Your college friend—someone you used to respect—posts self-righteous rants that make you embarrassed for them. The news feels like WWE wrestling, all performance and no substance. Politicians sound like cult leaders. Online discourse is a dumpster fire. And somehow, YOU'RE the weird one for thinking this is insane.

Every Thanksgiving becomes a minefield. Every Facebook scroll leaves you angry or exhausted. Every political conversation feels like shouting into a void where nobody listens, everyone performs, and nuance goes to die.

You're tired. You're disgusted. You want to unplug from all of it.

And you keep wondering: Am I crazy? Or is everyone else?

## Let Me Save You Some Anxiety

You're not crazy.

But you're also not fully seeing what's happening.

What you're experiencing isn't just "division" or "polarization." Those words are too clean, too bloodless. They don't capture the visceral disgust you feel when you see someone from "the other side." They don't explain why perfectly intelligent people believe absurd things. They don't account for why this keeps getting WORSE despite everyone claiming they want it to stop.

Here's what's actually happening: We're in a collective narcissism epidemic.

And social media is the virus vector.

## The Pattern You're Seeing

Let me describe what you've probably noticed but couldn't quite name:

**People used to disagree about politics. Now they treat it like religion.** Criticize their position and watch them react like you attacked their identity. Because you did.

**Both sides think they're the victims AND the heroes.** "We're under attack from [the left/the right], but we're morally superior so we'll win." Sound familiar? Now ask yourself: Can both sides be simultaneously the victim AND the aggressor?

**Everyone's an expert suddenly.** Your barista has strong opinions on monetary policy. Your dentist knows exactly how to fix geopolitics. Your cousin who failed high school biology has detailed views on vaccine development. And they're all CERTAIN. No doubt. No humility. Just righteousness.

**The moderates disappeared.** Where did they go? They didn't change their minds. They just got quiet. Because expressing any nuance gets you attacked from BOTH sides. The middle didn't vanish—it went into hiding.

**Compromise became betrayal.** Remember when politicians from different parties worked together and that was... normal? Now if you cooperate with "the enemy," your own tribe calls you a traitor. Purity spirals replaced pragmatism.

**Reality became negotiable.** We're not just disagreeing about values or policies anymore. We're disagreeing about basic facts. What used to be settled by evidence is now settled by which tribe you belong to.

Does this sound familiar? It should. Because you're living in it.

## The Exhausted Majority

Here's something that should give you hope: You're not alone.

Surveys show that about 70% of people are exhausted by polarization. They're tired of the tribal warfare. They don't want to pick a team and hate the other half of the country. They just want things to work.

That's most people. That's probably you.

But here's the problem: That exhausted 70% is SILENT. While the 15% on each extreme dominate every conversation, control the narrative, and make it seem like everyone is radicalized.

You know why you're silent? Because the cost of speaking up is too high. Say something nuanced and watch both sides descend on you. Try to bridge divides and get accused of "both-sidesing" or being a "coward" or "enabling fascism" or "virtue signaling."

So you shut up. You scroll. You feel the blood pressure rise. You close the app. And you wonder if you're the crazy one.

You're not.

## What's Actually Happening (Preview)

I'm going to tell you something that will sound extreme at first, but stick with me:

**We're not in a debate. We're in a narcissism pandemic.**

Individual narcissism is when someone has an inflated self-image, needs constant validation, lacks empathy, and reacts with rage when challenged. You know the type.

**Collective narcissism is the same thing, but at group level.**

"Our tribe is superior. Our beliefs are obviously correct. Anyone who disagrees is stupid or evil. We're under attack. We must defend ourselves. Compromise is weakness."

Sound familiar?

Here's the kicker: **Both sides show these patterns.** Left and right. Progressive and conservative. Your tribe and their tribe.

This isn't a "both sides are the same" argument. They're not. They disagree on substance, on values, on policy. That's normal. That's democracy.

But the WAY they disagree? The tribalism, the certainty, the dehumanization, the victim complex combined with superiority? That's narcissism. And it's everywhere.

## Why This Is Getting Worse

You might be thinking: "People have always been tribal. Politics has always been nasty. What's different now?"

What's different is the algorithm.

Social media platforms—Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, TikTok—are not neutral town squares. They're engagement-maximization machines. And they've discovered something interesting: **Outrage is the most engaging emotion.**

When you see something that makes you angry, you click. You share. You comment. You spend more time on the platform. More time = more ads = more money.

So the algorithm learns: Show people things that make them angry.

But here's where it gets dark: The algorithm doesn't just show you outrage. It shows you an escalating LADDER of outrage.

You watch one political video. The algorithm recommends a slightly more extreme version. You watch that. It recommends something even more extreme. Within weeks, you've gone from "I'm concerned about immigration policy" to "They're trying to destroy our country."

This isn't a bug. It's optimal performance. The algorithm is doing exactly what it was designed to do: maximize engagement.

And in doing so, it's feeding the narcissism pandemic.

## One Thing to Do Right Now

Before the next post, I want you to try something.

Open your social media feed—Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, whatever you use. Scroll for 5 minutes.

But this time, pay attention to how you FEEL.

- Are you angry?
- Do you feel superior to someone?
- Do you feel under attack?
- Do you feel righteousness?
