Part 2 of 12: The Narcissism Pandemic Series

Here's the thing about narcissism: The narcissist never thinks THEY'RE the narcissist. It's always the other guy.

The narcissistic boss thinks everyone else is incompetent. The narcissistic partner thinks they're the victim. The narcissistic parent thinks they're just being "honest" or "strong."

And collective narcissism? Same pattern, bigger scale.

Your tribe isn't crazy. THEIR tribe is crazy. Your tribe is righteous. THEIR tribe is evil. Your tribe is under attack. THEIR tribe is the aggressor.

Which is exactly what makes collective narcissism so dangerous. And why you probably can't see that you're in it.

What Is Collective Narcissism?

Let's start with individual narcissism, because you probably know what that looks like:

Individual Narcissist: - Grandiose self-image ("I'm special, superior, exceptional") - Needs constant validation ("Tell me I'm great") - Lacks empathy ("Your feelings don't matter") - Fragile ego (Criticism = existential threat) - Victim complex ("Everyone is against me") - Entitlement ("I deserve special treatment") - Rage when challenged ("How DARE you")

You know this person. Maybe you work with them. Maybe you're related to them. They're exhausting.

Now scale that up to a group. A tribe. A political movement.

Collective Narcissist: - "Our group is superior to others" - "We deserve recognition for our greatness" - "Other groups don't understand our struggles" - "Any criticism of us is an attack" - "We're simultaneously the best AND the most oppressed" - "Compromise with them is betrayal" - "They're not just wrong, they're evil"

Still sound familiar? It should.

Because both left and right political tribes in America (and Europe, and increasingly worldwide) show these exact patterns.

The Left's Version

Let me be specific so you can't dismiss this as abstract both-sidesing.

Progressive collective narcissism looks like:

"We're on the right side of history. We care about the oppressed, unlike them. We're fighting for justice. If you disagree with us, you're either ignorant or bigoted. There's no legitimate disagreement—either you get it or you're part of the problem. Our moral clarity is our strength. Anyone who questions our tactics is enabling fascism. We're under constant attack from reactionaries. Cancel culture isn't real, but also they deserve it. Tone policing is oppression. Civility is a tool of the powerful. If you're not with us, you're against us."

The narcissistic elements: - ✅ In-group superiority ("We're morally evolved") - ✅ Lack of empathy for out-group ("They're all complicit") - ✅ Victimhood + superiority simultaneously ("We're right AND oppressed") - ✅ Fragile ego (Criticism = Violence) - ✅ Purity spirals (Competitive virtue signaling) - ✅ Dehumanization ("Fascist," "Bootlicker," "Karen")

The Right's Version

And before anyone on the left feels validated, here's the mirror image:

Conservative collective narcissism looks like:

"We're the real Americans. We built this country. We work hard, unlike those entitled socialists. We're under attack from the woke mob. They're trying to destroy our values, our history, our way of life. The media is against us. The courts are against us. The education system is brainwashing our kids. We're the silent majority. We're the real victims of discrimination. They're not just wrong, they're trying to destroy Western civilization. Compromise is weakness. Strength is the only thing they understand. If you're not loyal, you're a RINO. America First. Our nation is exceptional. They hate us for our freedom."

The narcissistic elements: - ✅ In-group superiority ("We're the real patriots") - ✅ Lack of empathy for out-group ("They're all un-American") - ✅ Victimhood + superiority simultaneously ("We're great AND persecuted") - ✅ Fragile ego (Criticism = Treason) - ✅ Loyalty tests (Competitive patriotism) - ✅ Dehumanization ("Communist," "Snowflake," "NPC")

"But My Side Really IS Right"

I know what you're thinking right now.

If you lean left, you're thinking: "But conservatives really ARE enabling fascism / destroying the planet / oppressing minorities. This isn't narcissism, it's accurate."

If you lean right, you're thinking: "But progressives really ARE destroying traditional values / indoctrinating kids / undermining freedom. This isn't narcissism, it's reality."

And here's the trap: You're both right and both wrong.

Yes, there are real substantive disagreements. Climate change is real (sorry right). Biological sex matters (sorry left). Immigration policy is complex (sorry everyone who thinks it's simple). Systemic issues exist AND individual responsibility matters (sorry to ideologues on both sides).

But here's the key distinction:

Having strong political beliefs is not narcissism. Believing anyone who disagrees is evil or stupid? That's narcissism.

You can think conservative policies are wrong without thinking conservatives are monsters. You can think progressive tactics are counterproductive without thinking progressives are America-haters.

But collective narcissism makes that impossible. Because it requires that the other side be THE ENEMY. Not mistaken. Not prioritizing different values. Not operating from different assumptions.

Evil. Stupid. Dangerous. Other.

The Diagnostic Checklist

Want to know if you're caught in collective narcissism? Ask yourself these questions honestly:

1. Do you feel morally superior to the other side? - Not "I think they're wrong" - But "I'm a better person than them" 2. Do you struggle to empathize with why they believe what they believe? - Not "I understand but disagree" - But "I literally can't imagine how anyone could think that unless they're stupid/evil" 3. Do you feel like your group is under attack? - Not "they oppose my policy preferences" - But "they're trying to destroy us" 4. Does criticism of your side feel like a personal attack? - Not "I disagree with that critique" - But "How dare you criticize us when THEY'RE the problem" 5. Do you police your own side's loyalty? - Not "I disagree with that approach" - But "They're betraying the cause / giving ammunition to the enemy" 6. Do you feel righteous anger regularly? - Not occasional outrage at genuine injustice - But constant fury as your baseline emotional state 7. Do you dehumanize the other side even slightly? - Not "their policies would cause harm" - But "they're fascists/communists/sheep/NPCs"

If you answered yes to more than three of these, you're in the narcissism trap.

And before you think "but these questions prove I'm right to feel this way," that's the narcissism talking. The narcissist always has reasons why their narcissism is justified.

The Uncomfortable Truth

Here's what I need you to sit with:

Your tribe is not immune.

Whatever side you're on, whatever cause you fight for, however righteous your goals—you are susceptible to collective narcissism. You might already be exhibiting it.

This isn't moral equivalence. The substance of what you believe might be right. Your policy preferences might be correct. Your values might be better aligned with human flourishing.

But the WAY you hold those beliefs, the WAY you engage with disagreement, the WAY you treat people who don't agree—that can still be narcissistic.

And when it is, you make everything worse.

Why This Matters

You might be thinking: "Okay, so what? Politics is tribal. It's always been this way. Why does the label 'narcissism' matter?"

It matters because narcissism is a specific pathology with a specific cure.

If this were just normal political disagreement, we could solve it with better information, more dialogue, clearer communication. We've tried all that. It failed.

But if this is collective narcissism—and the data strongly suggests it is—then we need different interventions. We need to:

1. Disrupt the narcissistic supply (stop feeding the tribalism that gives people identity and meaning) 2. Treat the underlying wound (address the insecurity and fear that drives narcissistic defense) 3. Break the feedback loops (stop the systems that reinforce tribal behavior) 4. Build alternative identity (give people belonging that doesn't require enemies)

We'll get to all of that in later posts.

But first, you need to see the narcissism. In them, yes. But also in yourself.

The Test

Here's your homework before the next post:

Find a piece of political content you agree with. A tweet, an article, a video—something that made you nod along and think "yes, exactly."

Now ask: Does this content make me feel superior to someone?

If the answer is yes, you just identified narcissistic supply. The content isn't making you smarter or more informed. It's making you feel better about yourself by making you feel worse about them.

That's the algorithm feeding your tribal narcissism.

And it's doing the exact same thing to the other side.

One More Thing

I know this is uncomfortable. I know you want to argue with parts of this. I know you're thinking "but my side really is different."

Good. Sit with that discomfort.

Because the only way out of collective narcissism is through the painful recognition that we're all susceptible to it.

The narcissist never thinks they're the narcissist.

Unless they do the work of looking.

So look.
